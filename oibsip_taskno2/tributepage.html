ROSA PARKS

"Rosa Parks: Courageous catalyst of the civil rights movement."

About the Legend

☛ Early Life: Rosa Parks was born on February 4, 1913, in Tuskegee, Alabama, USA.

☛ Civil Rights Activism: She became known as the "mother of the civil rights movement" for her pivotal role in the Montgomery Bus Boycott.

☛ Montgomery Bus Boycott: On December 1, 1955, Parks refused to give up her seat to a white passenger on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama, sparking a 381-day boycott of the city's bus system.

☛ Arrest and Trial: Parks was arrested and charged with violating segregation laws, leading to a widely publicized trial.

☛ Leadership in the Boycott: Parks' action inspired and galvanized the African American community in Montgomery, with Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. emerging as a leader of the boycott.

☛ End of Segregation on Buses: The Supreme Court ruled in December 1956 that segregation on buses was unconstitutional, leading to the desegregation of public transportation.

☛ Continued Activism: Parks continued to be active in the civil rights movement, working for equality and justice throughout her life.

☛ Honors and Recognition: Parks received numerous awards and honors for her contributions to civil rights, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the Congressional Gold Medal.

☛ The Rosa Parks Act: In 1999, Michigan passed the Rosa Parks Act, which made it illegal to discriminate against people based on race, color, religion, or national origin in public accommodations.

☛ Books and Memoirs: Parks authored several books and memoirs, including "Rosa Parks: My Story" and "Quiet Strength."

☛ Legacy: Parks' bravery and activism are remembered as a turning point in the civil rights movement, inspiring future generations to stand up against injustice.

☛ Death and Commemoration: Rosa Parks passed away on October 24, 2005, in Detroit, Michigan. Her legacy lives on through monuments, memorials, and educational programs dedicated to civil rights and social justice.
